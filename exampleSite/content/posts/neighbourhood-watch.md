Starting a neighbourhood watch takes a few organised steps and a handful of committed neighbours.

## Talk to your neighbours first

Before any formal steps, have informal conversations on your street. Gauge interest and identify a few people willing to help coordinate.

## Contact your local police liaison

Most districts have a community police officer who supports neighbourhood watch programmes. They can provide guidance, signage, and registration support.

## Hold a founding meeting

Invite neighbours via a flyer or group chat. Keep the first meeting short: agree on a communication channel, assign a coordinator, and set expectations.

## Set up a communication channel

A group messaging app or a simple email list is sufficient. Agree on what kinds of incidents to report and how quickly.

## Register and get signs

Register your scheme with the relevant local authority or national programme. Official signs deter opportunistic crime and signal community cohesion.
